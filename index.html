Arthur P. Feeney

I am a Masters student studying Computer Science at the University of Massachusetts Amherst.

I have a general interest in Computer Science; but, of late, I have developed deeper interests in C++, data analysis, and approximation techniques. I enjoy working on projects that fit in the intersection of these topics. Two of my favorite side projects are a library for approximate inner product search and a library for making neural networks: Blimps Lib and nn.

Work and Research

For my undergraduate Senior Thesis, I had the privilege to do research in the intersection of neural networks and approximate search. This led to my first publication:

  • Feeney, Arthur and Yu Zhang. 2019. Convolution Acceleration: Query Based Filter Pruning. In Proceedings of the IEEE International Conference on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics, Bari, Italy, 6-9 October 2019

Prior internships: Texas HHSC, WGVS (Startup), Summer Research
